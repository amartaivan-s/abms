Agent-Based Modeling (ABM) is a bottom-up, microscale modeling approach where the system is described by individual agents (people, firms, particles, etc.) and their local interactions. From these simple rules emerge collective phenomena that cannot be easily predicted from top-down aggregate models. This section introduces a collection of interactive prototype models demonstrating the principles of agent-based modeling ‚Äî from diffusion processes and collective motion to decision-making and emergent phenomena.
