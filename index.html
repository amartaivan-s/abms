Why Agent‚ÄëBased Models for Diffusion?

Local Rules ‚Üí Global Patterns

Diffusion emerges from microscopic interactions: agents encounter neighbors, evaluate credibility/utility, and update their internal state. ABM captures heterogeneity, spatial embedding, and network effects that aggregate into macroscopic adoption curves and spatial waves.

  • Heterogeneity: varied thresholds, trust, risk, memory.
  • Topology: lattice grids, small‚Äëworld, scale‚Äëfree, empirical networks.
  • Context: media influence, influencers, institutional nudges.

Bridging Theory & Visualization

ABM complements analytical models (e.g., Bass, threshold models) by providing visual, scenario‚Äërich simulations to test what‚Äëif policies, seeding strategies, and robustness under uncertainty.

  • Reproduce S‚Äëcurves and tipping points.
  • Map spatial frontiers of adoption.
  • Compare policies: seeding hubs vs. random seeding.

Modeling Patterns

State Machines

Agents carry a state and memory:

  • Rumor: Unaware (U) ‚Üí Aware (A) ‚Üí Sharing (S) ‚Üí Dormant (D).
  • Innovation: Non‚Äëadopter (N) ‚Üí Adopter (Y).

Transitions depend on neighbor counts, credibility weights, thresholds, and exogenous inputs (media).

  • Bass model: dA/dt = p (M ‚àí A) + q A (M ‚àí A)/M
  • Linear threshold: adopt if ‚àë w_i x_i ‚â• Œ∏
  • Independent cascade: each adopter has one chance to activate a neighbor with prob p

ABM can reproduce or deviate from these baselines depending on topology and heterogeneity, enabling hypothesis testing.

Mesa Implementation Notes

  • Use MultiGrid for spatial embedding; CanvasGrid for live visualization.
  • Scheduler: RandomActivation or SimultaneousActivation for diffusion fairness.
  • DataCollector: track adoption rate, frontier size, cluster count, and R(t).
  • Seed strategies: random, central, high‚Äëdegree (if using network), or targeted cells.

Metrics & Outputs

  • Temporal: adoption curve, peak sharing time, time‚Äëto‚Äë50%.
  • Spatial: Moran‚Äôs I, cluster size distribution, wavefront speed.
  • Policy: ROI of seeding strategies; sensitivity to thresholds and media.
